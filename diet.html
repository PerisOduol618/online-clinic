Patient Safety

With a growing aging population people are receiving health care at home,thus there might be safety issues in their environment therefore several actions can be taken to make home as safe place as possible.

Providing necessary supplies and Equipment

Unlike care in hospitalor clinic,the purchase of basic medical supplies may be responssibilty of the patient when care is provided at home

Talk to our team to make sure you have all necessary supplies

Refill medicines before they run out of dosage

Keep readily available hand sanitizers and other supplies needed to stop the sread of germs

If syringes or needles are used for care,provide sharps container for proper disposal


Preventing falls

common causes of injuries in the home is falling,to reduce the chances of falling one should;

Check the house for hazards

Install adequet lighting

Install handrails and hand grap where needed

Use proper footware

Tell health care about your previous falls

Make the floors nonslip

Move more carefully

Clean up clutter

Improving Air Quality

Respiratory illness is a common medical problem among those receiving home health care improving air quality is good for everyone's health and especially those with breathing problems

Eliminate smoke

clean surfaces of dust

control Mold

Replace filters

Provide workig fire alarms and carbon monoxide detectors

Invest air purifier


COVID

Protect yourself and others from COVID-19

Wash your hands often with soap and water atleast 20 seconds after being in public place ,or use a hand sanitizer with at least 60% alcohol

Avoid touching your eyes,nose and mouth with unwashed hands

Avoid close contact with people who are sick and practice social distance atleast 6 feet away from others.

Wear mask to cover your mouth and nose

Cover your nose and mouth with tissue when coughing or sneezing and throw away the tissue after using

Clean and disinfect frequently touched surfaces daily
